Meet Hobo Joe!

Hobo Joe is a long-haired (mostly) black cat that loves to lounge around. At nine years old, he's not very energetic, so he finds most of his entertainment in watching other cats from afar, sitting in sunny windows, and trying to find ways to get outside.

Joe had spent part of his life as, well, a hobo, wandering around a neighborhood in a small rural town in Georgia. He was a hunter and brought my family dead snakes, squirrels, and lizards as gifts. He essentially claimed our house and would lay on our porch, sometimes cuddling with the kittens of one of the female strays. He officially became our cat, though, when we decided to bring him inside after someone tried to hurt him.

For a while, he was an indoor and outdoor cat, even after we moved from Georgia to Florida. However, once we moved again to our current home, Joe went outside once and was terrified of the neighborhood. My mom brought him back inside to let him cool, but a couple of days later we learned that a lot of the strays in the area had FIV, so we kept him in until we could get him vaccinated. After he got his shots, we tried letting him back out, but he had gotten comfortable in the house and was too scared to go back out. From then, he's just been an indoor cat, though lately, he has been trying to escape outside.
